Ending 1: Neutral

Andrey and Dimka get home safe and sound. Andrey realizes that the shift supervisor is involved in something dark, possibly related to ancient beliefs and the creature they saw in the forest. They decide to leave Zarechye as soon as possible without telling anyone what happened. Karachun remains in the taiga, and Andrey takes with him a heavy knowledge that ancient evil has not disappeared.

Ending 2: Bad

The ritual fails catastrophically. The ancient entity invades Andrey's mind and body, his pleas for release futile. His eyes become chillingly luminous, his voice a distorted echo. Karachun fully possesses him, extinguishing Andrey's humanity and turning him into a vessel for its malevolent will, ready to unleash its icy reign.

Ending 3: Good

The ritual is a success. Andrey's heartfelt incantation, combined with Taisiya's ancient knowledge and the power of their combined will, forces Karachun back into the shadows. The storm subsides, and a sense of peace returns to the land. The immediate threat to Dimka is lifted. Exhausted but triumphant, Andrey and Dimka, guided by Taisiya's wisdom, manage to leave the cursed lands. They return home safely, forever changed by their encounter with the ancient darkness, but whole and together.
